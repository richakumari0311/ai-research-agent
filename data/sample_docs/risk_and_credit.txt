Credit Risk and Loan Portfolio Intelligence India 2025

Microfinance Sector Stress Indicators:
The microfinance industry in India reported rising delinquency rates in H2 FY2025.
Portfolio at Risk greater than 30 days rose to 9.4 percent from 4.8 percent a year ago.
States with highest stress: Assam, Tamil Nadu, Odisha, and West Bengal showing 12 to 18 percent PAR30.
Key reason: overleveraging with borrowers holding average 4.2 active loans simultaneously.
RBI issued advisory to all NBFC-MFIs to cap borrower indebtedness at INR 2 lakh across all lenders.

Credit Card Market Trends:
Total credit cards in circulation crossed 107 million in March 2025.
Monthly credit card spends reached INR 1.87 lakh crore in March 2025.
Delinquency rate on credit cards at 2.8 percent, up from 1.9 percent two years ago.
HDFC Bank holds 23 percent market share, followed by SBI Cards at 19 percent and ICICI Bank at 17 percent.
Co-branded cards in partnerships with e-commerce players growing at 55 percent year on year.

Corporate Bond Market:
Total corporate bond issuance in FY2025 was INR 9.7 lakh crore, a record high.
AAA rated issuances dominated at 68 percent of total volume.
Infrastructure and renewable energy companies were the largest issuers.
Yield spreads on BBB rated bonds compressed by 45 basis points indicating improving risk appetite.
Foreign portfolio investors now hold 4.2 percent of outstanding corporate bonds, up from 1.8 percent two years ago.

AI in Credit Risk Assessment:
Leading banks like ICICI, Axis, and Kotak now use ML models for real time credit scoring.
Alternative data sources used include GST filings, UPI transaction history, and social media signals.
AI based underwriting reduced loan processing time from 5 days to 4 hours at major banks.
Default prediction accuracy improved by 34 percent compared to traditional bureau score only models.
Regulatory requirement: all AI credit models must be explainable and auditable as per RBI circular 2024.
